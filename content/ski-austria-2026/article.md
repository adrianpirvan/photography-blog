# Crossing Austrian Mountains, | One Lift at a Time

**Category:** Travel
**Date:** Jan '26

---

Thanks to the excellent life planning of being born in January, someone gets a yearly birthday ski trip. This time we tried Austria — Kaprun, with its Kitzsteinhorn glacier, which appeared very convincing.

The slopes themselves weren’t exactly the highlight — a bit stop-and-go, not much flow — but the views from the top made it difficult to stay disappointed for very long.

Luckily the ski pass covers three areas, including Saalbach with about 270 km of slopes, which changed the whole game. One lift, one ridge, a valley crossing, another cable car. Soon we’ve crossed the entire region without skiing the same slope twice. And along the way: more views! And when the sun plays along, the mountains fade into blue layers, cable cars float through the haze, and suddenly everything looks cinematic.
