# A Room That Had | No Right to Be Beautiful

**Category:** Life
**Date:** 20/03/26

---

I've never really been into music. I say this not as a confession, but as a statement of fact; the same way some people don't like cilantro. It just doesn't do much for me.

So what does this completely useless piece of personal trivia have to do with anything? Well, the other day I found myself at George's gig with his new band. But I wasn't just there to listen. I was there to photograph it. Obviously, the role came with its perks: backstage access, free catering, exclusive time with the artists. Ok fine, I admit, that last bit was just my imagination. In fact, think small, the backstage was the stage and vice versa, only friends and family, 50 people max, the kind where you recognise half of the crowd and the other half are clearly someone's mum.

And so I didn't get free snacks, but I did get something quieter and, in hindsight, more valuable: a reason to be in the room differently. Camera in hand, I wasn't just attending; I started watching. Really watching. Every face, every moment, the bit of light that falls exactly right but usually doesn't. It makes you present in a way that's almost unfair to everyone else.

The location? Oh boy! What a place! 
You know that super weird, graffiti-covered underground place that gives you the creeps during the day, and even more creeps at night? That! The kind of place that makes you check twice where the exit is. It was an old, abandoned petrol station, De Roze Tanker, the Pink Tanker, which, for your geographical reference, sits directly across the road from The Yellow Pump. You're welcome! As it turns out, a "venue" like that doesn't just host a gig, it becomes part of it.

And then there was the music. In that room, a gentle unease, a subtle feeling of trespassing, low ceiling, painted walls, and a crowd that had shown up out of love for something small, unsponsored and real. Everything clicked. It was impossible not to feel it. Not just hear the music. Feel it.

I've been to very few concerts in my life, and I remember almost none of them. I'll remember this one.
Maybe it was the place. Maybe it was having a camera and a reason to pay attention. Maybe it was just the right song at the right moment in a room that had no right to be beautiful.

Or maybe, every once in a while, life quietly dismantles one of your long-held opinions about yourself. And you no longer fight it. You just "capture" it. 

The humans behind all of this: [Ghostwood Ensemble](https://ghostwoodensemble.bandcamp.com/album/ghostwood-ensemble)
